The Diagnosis

Your volatility model is broken.

Institutional allocation is driven by mean-variance optimization. The process is straightforward: estimate volatility, estimate returns, optimize. The problem is not the process. The problem is the volatility input.

Standard models measure Bitcoin's volatility as the standard deviation of returns, assuming a normal distribution. Bitcoin does not follow a normal distribution. It follows a power law — the same mathematical pattern governing network effects, city growth, and systems that compound over centuries.

When you measure deviation from the wrong baseline, you get the wrong volatility. When you get the wrong volatility, the optimizer produces the wrong allocation. The 2% number is not conservative. It is the output of a measurement error.

The Strategy

Kelly criterion meets power law.

The Kelly criterion provides the mathematically optimal position size when expected returns and variance are known. Applied to Bitcoin with power law-adjusted inputs, it produces a dynamic allocation that responds to where price sits relative to the trend:

f* = μ / σ²
Where μ = trend growth + mean reversion − risk-free rate, and σ² = variance of annual returns.
